Teaching the Machine

Training the Model

We retrieved training data from a basketball scrape library and processed the data into our model. We chose our model parameters based on our understanding of activation functions and experimenting with hidden layers. Tanh was chosen as the hidden layer activation function because our data is non-linear and categorical, with softmax as the out activation function because our outcome is binary. Two hidden layers are used because adding additional layers didn't yield lower data loss or higher model accuracy.

Predicting the Winner

Our trained model is saved and loaded into our prediction code. The data for user's prediction input is retrieved from a csv of a team's past performance from the previous basketball season. The performance data is queried using list and for loop functions, in which the user's chosen team names are searched in the csv and returns the respective data row. The specific variables are parsed based on the respective csv column and organized into an array that is the same array as the training data. The array is inputted in the model and the prediction outcome is re-labeled as a string.
